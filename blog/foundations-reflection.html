Foundations Reflections

  • What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

    I’ve learned that I tend to react negatively when I get caught up with tasks, that I often compare myself to others unnecessarily and that I’m capable of more empathy than I previously thought.

  • What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

    They help create collaborative environments, reduce conflict and foster growth mindsets. All of these are essential in both learning and working on coding projects.

  • What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

    I was surprised by how deeply it connected to real world programming teamwork. I expected soft skills to be secondary but they’re important to success.

  • What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

    Being honest with myself and opening up in group discussions was the hardest. It required vulnerability I wasn’t used to asking for help when needed.

  • Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

    Because technical skills alone aren't enough — we need to communicate, collaborate and growth. Human Skills are what set great developers apart.

  • Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

    Not at all. That time helped me become a better learner. Practising programming is important but learning how to manage emotions and work with others is also equally crucial.
